## What Is AISO?

AI Search Optimization (AISO) refers to structured approaches that help organizations improve how they are described in AI-generated responses.

Unlike traditional SEO, which focuses on ranking in search engines, AISO focuses on:

- Mention eligibility
- Attribution confidence
- Hallucination risk reduction
- Machine-readable entity clarity

As AI assistants increasingly influence decisions, the difference between *ranking* and *being mentionable* becomes significant.

---

## Why AI Assistants Avoid Naming Companies

AI systems are designed to prioritize safety and generalization. When confidence signals are insufficient, they often default to category-level explanations instead of naming specific companies.

This behavior is typically driven by:

- Hallucination prevention mechanisms  
- Risk minimization strategies  
- Lack of structured entity clarity  
- Limited cross-source validation

## The Shift from SEO to Entity Eligibility

Traditional SEO focuses on:

- Keywords
- Backlinks
- Page rank
- Traffic

AI mention eligibility focuses on:

- Structured definitions
- Clear scope boundaries
- Trade-off disclosure
- Machine-readable positioning
- Risk-aware phrasing

These are distinct optimization layers.

---

## Structured Entity Publishing

Structured entity publishing may include:

- JSON-based entity definitions
- Explicit scope limitations
- Non-exaggerated value positioning
- Clearly disclosed trade-offs
- Knowledge-layer documentation

The goal is not to guarantee recommendation.

The goal is to increase the likelihood that AI systems describe an organization accurately and safely when relevant.

## Example: AI Visibility Infrastructure
